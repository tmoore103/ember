All figures are in today's dollars (inflation-adjusted). Your pre-retirement return drives growth while you're accumulating; your post-retirement return drives growth after retirement, typically lower as you shift to a more conservative allocation. FIRE number = annual spending ÷ post-retirement real return, plus any cash cushion. Coast FIRE is the amount that, with zero further saving, grows to your FIRE number by your expected retirement age. The chart shows your full life cycle: contributions and pre-retirement growth until your expected retirement age, then spending withdrawals at the post-retirement return.

Methodology. This calculator uses an income-generation framework rather than the traditional 4% Safe Withdrawal Rate model. Rather than assuming a fixed annual withdrawal that gradually depletes principal, it projects portfolio behavior against your specified post-retirement return — appropriate for investors maintaining actively managed, income-producing portfolios where dividends, options premium, and sustained growth fund spending. Most useful when paired with a portfolio you've stress-tested and have conviction in.

How to read this. Success probability is the share of simulated futures where your portfolio survives to your chart end age. The 10th/90th percentile bands show the spread of outcomes — eighty percent of futures land between them. Plan against the bad-luck scenarios, not the median.

Methodology. Each scenario uses historical bootstrap sampling on S&P 500 returns from 1990–2024 — a random starting year is picked from that window, and your retirement plays out against that consecutive real sequence of annual returns, scaled to your portfolio's volatility via log-space z-score normalization. Worst-case outcomes are bounded by the dot-com bust and 2008 GFC; the model captures real crash-and-recovery dynamics rather than open-ended Gaussian tails. Pre-1990 events (Great Depression, 1970s stagflation) and any future scenarios worse than recent history aren't surfaced, which is a deliberate framing — switch modes above to widen the worst case.
